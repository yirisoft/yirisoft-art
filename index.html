01 About

Designing for Players.
Thinking Like One.

I'm Monica Mae Dizon — a Game UX/UI Designer with 7+ years of experience crafting interfaces that disappear into the experience.

I specialize in designing for games: the unique challenge of building UI that serves real-time decision making, emotional pacing, and player progression — all without breaking immersion. I've worked across mobile, PC, and console platforms, collaborating with cross-functional teams of developers, artists, producers, and narrative designers to ship polished, player-centered experiences.

My process starts with understanding how players think, not just how they click. I use research, rapid iteration, and a deep obsession with game feel to build interfaces that are clear under pressure, satisfying to navigate, and aligned with the visual identity of each game's world.

I've contributed to 8 shipped titles across mobile RPGs, PC strategy, and console action-adventure. Whether embedded in a studio team or brought in as a specialist consultant, I show up ready to solve hard design problems — and make the game better for the people who play it.

HUD · Real-time Systems

Combat HUD Redesign

Project Nightfall · Yirisoft Studios · PC & Console — 2024

The Challenge

Players were losing track of critical status effects mid-combat. The HUD needed a full information-hierarchy rebuild without adding visual noise.

My Role

Lead UX/UI Designer · Researcher · Prototyper

What I Designed

  • HUD layout and real-time feedback system
  • Status effect and cooldown visual language
  • Damage and threat directionality indicators
  • Accessibility options for colorblind and low-vision players
